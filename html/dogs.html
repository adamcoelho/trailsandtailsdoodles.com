Doodle Generations

F1 Generations

A First Generation (F1) Goldendoodle is a Golden Retriever crossed with a Poodle (50% Poodle, 50% Golden Retriever). F1 puppies have a low to non-shedding coat. F1 Goldendoodles are usually tolerable for people with mild allergies. People that are allergic to animals are often allergic to the dander (dead skin that is continually shed). No studies have proven that any dog is 100% hypo-allergenic. We generally see more of the Retriever personality in these puppies than in the F1B puppies.

Maiva is our purebred English Cream Retriever and when she is mated with our red Moyen stud, Rio, they produce F1 puppies. We are thrilled that Rio's red color is dominant! Their puppies will have wavy coats.

F1B Generations

An F1B Goldendoodle is an F1 Goldendoodle bred with a Poodle. F1B puppies are 75% Poodle and 25% Retriever, which makes this generation the most hypo-allergenic of all the Goldendoodle generations we breed. The F1B Goldendoodles have beautiful wavy to curly plush coats.

Ginger is our F1 Goldendoodle. My puppies that are the result of the mating of Ginger and my red Moyen stud, Rio, are F1B Goldendoodles. They typically have soft curly coats.

Hybrid Vigor

When two unrelated breeds of dogs are mated, the first generation of puppies are referred to as hybrids, thus Ginger is a hybrid. Hybrid puppies have what is called hybrid vigor and have less health problems than either purebred parent.
